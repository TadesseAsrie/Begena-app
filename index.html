About the Begena (በገና)

History and Origin

The Begena is one of the oldest stringed instruments in the world, widely recognized as an spiritual pillar of Ethiopian musical heritage. According to sacred tradition, it is linked directly to the biblical King David, leading to its designation as the "Harp of David." Historically used exclusively within religious contexts, Christian meditation, and imperial courts, it offers an aesthetic radically distinct from secular festive instruments like the Krar.

Structural Dynamics and Tuning

Constructed typically with 10 heavy gut strings stretching across a large rectangular wooden resonator box, the Begena stands close to a meter tall. Uniquely, small pieces of leather or wood called Gizit are positioned between each string and the wide bridge. This creates an intentional, distinct buzzing hum upon plucking, serving as a continuous acoustic drone loaded with overtones.

Cultural Value

Unlike instruments geared towards polyphonic complexity or high-tempo dancing rhythms, the Begena demands patient deceleration. Its traditional scales (primarily Tizita Minor or dedicated spiritual sub-modes) promote profound concentration, scriptural prayer rendering, and meditative isolation.
